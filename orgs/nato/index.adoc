== Overview
The North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) is a political and military alliance
founded in 1949 by the North Atlantic Treaty (Washington Treaty). NATO’s core
purpose is collective defence, expressed most prominently through Article 5 of
the Treaty, alongside crisis management and cooperative security. footnote:[NATO, “Founding treaty” (Washington Treaty), signed 4 April 1949.]

== Core functions
* Collective defence planning and deterrence posture coordination among Allies.
* Operational command structure and mission support (as agreed by Allies).
* Political consultation and coordination through NATO’s principal councils and committees.

== Governance structure
*Decision-making bodies:* North Atlantic Council (NAC) as the principal political decision-making body; Military Committee as the senior military authority; supporting committees and integrated civil-military staffs. footnote:[NATO public organizational descriptions.]
*Leadership:* Secretary General (international civilian head), supported by the International Staff and International Military Staff. footnote:[NATO public organizational descriptions.]
*Funding model:* Common-funded civil and military budgets and the NATO Security Investment Programme (NSIP), alongside much larger national defence budgets funded by individual member states. footnote:[NATO, “Funding NATO”.]
